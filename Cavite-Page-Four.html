Religions

Of the 108,667,043 household population in 2020, nearly four fifths or 85,645,362 persons (78.8%) reported Roman Catholic as their religious affiliation. It was followed by Islam with 6,981,710 persons (6.4%), and Iglesia ni Cristo with 2,806,524 persons (2.6%). In 2015, these were also the top three religious affiliations in the country. Roman Catholic


Roman Catholic faith in Cavite is deeply rooted, overseen by the Diocese of Imus with over 3 million members, 90+ parishes, and 8+ diocesan shrines. Key historic sites include the Imus Cathedral, Silang's 1639 Church of Our Lady of Candelaria, and Naic's 17th-century church, with major devotions to Our Lady of the Pillar and Our Lady of Solitude of Porta Vaga.

Roman Catholicism: The most prevalent religion in Cavite, with the province hosting a significant number of Catholic churches and adhering to the patroness, Our Lady of Porta Vaga.


Iglesia Ni Cristo

Iglesia ni Cristo: A well-known indigenous Philippine church with a significant following in the region.


Islam

the Muslim population in the province of Cavite is 34,969, representing approximately 0.81% of the total population in the region.
